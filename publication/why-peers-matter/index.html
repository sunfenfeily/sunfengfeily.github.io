Why Peers Matter: How Friendship Network Affects Students' Academic Performance

August 2020
PDF

Abstract

Social relationships affect behavior and performance. Based on the student friendship questionnaire surveys conducted in high school classes, this article examines the influence of network position of students within the class on their academic performance. Our research shows that the more popular students are in class, the better academic performance he or she might get in the exams. However, the number of friends a student filled out in the survey seemingly have little correlation with their scores. In addition, this paper tests the heterogeneous impact of social network on school performance and further attempts to analyze the relevant mechanisms. In this way, we provide a rigorous empirical study to prove that difference in the direction of social network relationships affects behavioral performance, which indicates that students can promote the accumulation of human capital from an early stage through constructing effective social network in their daily life.
